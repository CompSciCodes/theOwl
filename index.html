Code2College

It is highly recommended that Computer Science students participate in the after school Code2College program, however, this program is offered to any student who is interested in coding! The knowledge gained helps prepare for future job markets in coding careers, focusing on professional behaviors and specific industry-standard skills.

Coding sessions occur after school on Monday/Wednesday or Tuesday/Thursday. Monthly Saturday workshops allow students to meet industry professionals, and the hosting company provides breakfast and lunch to all attendees. Students can receive scholarships and summer internships through the program.

For more information on this fantastic opportunity, visit the Code2College website. If you are interested in participating, please see Ms. Jansen in room B205. You can also email tina.jansen@dvisd.net with your questions.

Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (DV-SHPE)

This club partners up with the University of Texas SHPE, who visit with the Del Valle members to share their college experiences. The organization focuses on Engineering professions in particular, and helps broaden student knowledge of topics like time management, class schedules, and university life.

DV-SHPE visits the UT campus to experience a day in the life of a college student. This opportunity allows DV students to explore various career options, participate in engineering learning labs, and see the Cockrell School of Engineering facilities. Senior members can apply to receive a SHPE stole to wear at graduation.

For more information on this prestigious club, visit the UT-SHPE website. If you are interested in participating, please see Mr. Casares in room C105. You can also email derek.casares@dvisd.net with your questions.

University Interscholastic League (UIL)

This league is not for the faint of heart as its members compete against the best of Texas public schools in academics! Competitions include various challenges in STEM, Journalism, and other electives listed below:

SCI-TECH-ENG-MATH JOURNALISM MISCELLANEOUS
Number Sense Ready Writing Current Events
Calculator Applications Headline Writing Social Studies
Computer Applications Literary Criticism Spelling and Vocabulary
Computer Science News Writing Accounting
Mathematics Feature Writing Debate
Science Copy Editing Extemporaneous Speaking
Robotics Editorial Writing Oral Interpretation

Practices are conducted after school with a teacher/coach of your subject, so dates and locations will vary. Competitions occur mostly on Saturdays at various high school locations. Students have opportunities to compete at district, regional, and state levels, and can also earn scholarships. If you are a student who loves academics and possesses an innate desire to challenge yourself, then this league is for you!

For more information on this fun and intellectual program, visit the UIL website. If you are interested in participating, please see Mr. Plumley in room D201 or Mr. Rasp in portable H90. You can also email louis.plumley@dvisd.net or jeffrey.rasp@dvisd.net with your questions.
